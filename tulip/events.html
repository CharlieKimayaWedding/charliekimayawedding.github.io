Mehndi, otherwise known as henna, is an artistic ritual of applying henna paste to the body in intricate patterns, leaving a dark stain which signifies the groom's depth of love for the bride! Traditionally, bridal mehndi is applied to the hands, arms and feet. As part of our pre-wedding celebrations, we will be having an informal mehndi party to celebrate this ritual and a chance for guests to have mehndi applied to their hands if they wish.

The haldi (turmeric) ceremony, also known as a pithi, involves the application of turmeric paste on to the bride and groom by the guests, as a gesture of blessing the couple prior to the wedding.

A Hindu ceremony is a religious ceremony composed of several different rituals involving the bride, the groom and their families. It is led by a Pandit (priest). This ceremony usually takes 1 - 1.5 hours.

The civil ceremony is a legally binding marriage performed by a registrar from the local council. This ceremony involves exchanging vows and signing the wedding certificate in front of two official witnesses and the guests. This ceremony usually takes 20 - 30 minutes.

The sangeet is a celebration of the union of not only the couple, but also of the two families; akin to the evening reception. Typically, choreographed dances are performed by both families.

The wedding breakfast is the main meal that follows the civil ceremony, traditionally the first meal as a legally married couple.
